Ho Chi Minh

The man who liberated the Vietnamese people.

Here's a time line of Ho Chi Minh's life:

  • 1890-1911 - Born in 1890 into a patriotic family in Nghe An, Ho Chi Minh (originally Nguyễn Sinh Cung, later Nguyễn Tất Thành and Nguyễn Ái Quốc) developed a strong sense of patriotism early on. Witnessing the harsh realities of French colonial rule and the failures of existing resistance movements, he became convinced that a new revolutionary path was needed to liberate Vietnam. Driven by an intense desire to save his country, he decided to travel abroad to learn from other nations before returning to lead his people to independence.
  • 1911-1920 - In 1911, Ho Chi Minh left Vietnam for France to pursue his revolutionary aspirations. He traveled extensively across continents from 1911 to 1917, observing the suffering under colonialism and realizing that exploited people worldwide shared a common struggle against imperialist oppressors. In 1919, as Nguyễn Ái Quốc, he joined the French Socialist Party and submitted the "Demands of the Annamese People" to the Versailles Conference, advocating for Vietnamese rights and emphasizing self-reliance for liberation. A pivotal moment came in July 1920 when he read Lenin's "Theses on the National and Colonial Questions," which profoundly clarified the path to liberation for Vietnam through the proletarian revolution. This led him to embrace Leninism, becoming the first Vietnamese communist and forging a powerful synthesis of patriotism, internationalism, and the pursuit of national independence aligned with socialism.
  • 1920-1930 - From 1922, Ho Chi Minh, as Nguyễn Ái Quốc, actively fought against colonialism. He co-founded the Union of Colonial Peoples and launched Le Paria to expose colonial injustices. In 1925, his book French Colonialism on Trial further denounced French rule. He established the Vietnamese Revolutionary Youth League in Guangzhou, training cadres and publishing Thanh Niên to disseminate Marxist-Leninist ideas, which were codified in his 1927 work, Đường Cách mệnh. He consistently emphasized the need for a strong political organization to lead the revolution. In 1930, he unified three communist groups to form the Communist Party of Vietnam. The Party's first Political Platform, largely drafted by him, creatively applied Marxism-Leninism to Vietnam's unique context, marking a crucial turning point that guided the nation towards independence and socialism and ended the crisis in revolutionary leadership.
  • 1930-1945 - The Communist Party of Vietnam, founded in 1930, immediately led the 1930-1931 revolutionary wave, including the Nghe Tinh Soviets, with Ho Chi Minh coordinating international support and criticizing Party weaknesses. Although the Party briefly changed its name to the "Indochinese Communist Party" in October 1930 following Comintern directives, Ho Chi Minh maintained significant influence, continuing his studies at the Lenin International School. After years of revolutionary work abroad, enduring hardships, he returned to Vietnam on January 28, 1941. Under his leadership, the Eighth Central Committee Conference in May 1941 prioritized national liberation, establishing the Viet Minh Front on May 19, 1941, and later the Vietnam Propaganda and Liberation Army (December 22, 1944). His call for a General Uprising on August 18, 1945, led to the successful August Revolution, overthrowing colonial and feudal rule. Finally, on September 2, 1945, Ho Chi Minh delivered the Declaration of Independence, establishing the Democratic Republic of Vietnam and affirming the nation's right to freedom and independence, a vision he had laid out since the Party's 1930 platform.
  • 1945-1969 - After gaining power, President Ho Chi Minh led the Vietnamese people to overcome many difficulties, fighting against the invasion of French colonialism and then American imperialism. He combined armed struggle with skillful diplomacy, consolidated the government, built the Party, and launched a long-term resistance war for all people. He proposed the path of building socialism in the North, fighting to liberate the South, and unifying the country. In his Will before he passed away (1969), he affirmed his belief in the victory of the nation and called for the building of a peaceful, independent, democratic, and prosperous Vietnam. His life and career are a shining example for generations to follow.
